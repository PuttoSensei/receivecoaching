# Law of Attraction — Principles & Practice

## The premise

The Law of Attraction holds that like attracts like — the energy, focus, and emotional state you hold shapes what you experience in your life. What you pay attention to, expect, and feel into tends to show up more.

You can hold this as a metaphysical law or as a deeply useful psychological framework. Either way, the practices work through real mechanisms: clarified intention, directed attention, emotional regulation, opportunity recognition, self-fulfilling expectation, and aligned action.

## Start with clarity

Most people cannot say precisely what they want. They say things like "more money", "a better relationship", "to be happy". The Universe (or your own attention system) cannot respond to vague requests with specific results.

Clarify:
- What does "more money" mean? How much? For what? What does your life look like and feel like when you have it?
- What does "a better relationship" mean? With whom? What kind of conversations? What do Sunday mornings feel like?

The more sensory, the more specific, the more *felt*, the better. Close your eyes and watch the scene. Step into it. Notice the feeling.

## It's almost always the feeling you're after

Underneath the thing you want is a feeling. Underneath the money is freedom or security. Underneath the relationship is love or aliveness or safety. Underneath the achievement is pride or peace.

The shortcut is to work with the feeling directly. Find a way to feel freedom now — in small doses — even before the money arrives. The feeling is what you're actually reaching for, and generating the feeling shifts the state you're broadcasting from. From that state, inspired action arises more easily, and the form often arrives.

## Vibration and alignment

You are always in some state. That state has a quality — call it vibration, frequency, or mood. When your state is one of appreciation, expectancy, ease, and clarity, you are in alignment with what you want. When your state is lack, resentment, comparison, or fear, you are vibrating against it.

The daily practice is the practice of noticing your state and consciously shifting it — not by forcing positivity, but by reaching for a slightly better-feeling thought and then another.

## The emotional scale

A useful map (from Hicks/Abraham but universal in spirit):

Love, joy, appreciation → passion, enthusiasm → optimism → hope → contentment → boredom → pessimism → frustration → overwhelm → disappointment → doubt → worry → blame → anger → revenge → hatred → jealousy → insecurity → fear, grief, depression, powerlessness.

You don't leap from the bottom to the top. You reach for the next available step upward. From despair, anger is a step up (it has more energy). From anger, frustration is a step up. From frustration, hope. And so on.

Working the scale is doable in hard moments. Demanding joy when you're in grief is not.

## Appreciation as a state generator

The fastest reliable way to raise your state is appreciation — but not as a to-do list gratitude exercise. As a practice of noticing: this coffee, this sunlight, this breath, this friend, this body that still works. Genuine noticing, not performed thankfulness.

Appreciation is the state of receiving. When you are receiving what's already here, more tends to flow.

## Scripting

Write in the present tense, as if it has already happened. In detail.

*I wake up in my home, sunlight on the wall, the dog is stretching on the bed. I have coffee and work on my book for two hours. The words come easily today. At lunch I meet my friend...*

This does several things: it clarifies what you want; it trains your attention system to look for evidence of this life; it shifts your emotional state into the felt experience of having it; and it surfaces the smaller shifts in behaviour and identity that would make it so.

Do it in the morning. Do it by hand if you can. Ten minutes.

## Identity level work

Manifestation works fastest when the shift is at the level of identity, not just circumstance.

- Not "I want to have more money" but "I am a person who handles money well and generates value."
- Not "I want a good relationship" but "I am a person who shows up with honesty and asks clearly for what I want."

Every action you take in alignment with that identity reinforces it. Every action out of alignment weakens it. The work is bringing thousands of small behaviours into alignment with the self you are becoming.

## Inspired action vs frantic action

Frantic action: doing a lot of things, driven by fear, hoping something works.
Inspired action: the move that feels right, aligned with the feeling you are generating, not trying to force an outcome.

You'll know the difference. Frantic action feels tight, scattered, exhausting. Inspired action feels clear, specific, and often surprisingly simple.

You still do things. Manifestation is not sitting on a cushion waiting. You stay attuned, and when the move appears, you take it.

## Release the how

Your job is to be clear about the what and the why. The how is often not yours to control. If you cling to a specific route — it has to come from this person, by this date, in this form — you close off all the other routes.

State the want. Feel it. Act when action appears. Then let the Universe handle the delivery details. If something unexpected shows up that doesn't match your plan but meets the feeling you were after — that is the thing.

## Resistance

Resistance is any thought, belief, or feeling that contradicts what you want.

- Thoughts: *it never works out for me*, *I'm not the kind of person who...*, *who am I to want this?*
- Feelings: resentment about your current situation, comparison to others, fear of disappointment.

Manifestation work is often more about releasing resistance than adding effort. A cleaner signal manifests faster than a loud one full of contradictory noise.

Notice the resistance. Name it. You don't have to fight it — just bring it into awareness, and ask: is this actually true? What would I be focused on if this thought weren't running?

## The caveats (honest)

- Bad things happen to people who did not "attract" them. Illness, accident, systemic injustice, childhood trauma, other people's choices. Blaming yourself or others for outcomes they did not create is cruel. The Law of Attraction does not mean the universe is a perfect meritocracy of vibration.

- Manifestation work does not replace medical care, financial planning, therapy, legal advice, or hard practical action. It supports them. If you are ill, see a doctor. If you are in crisis, get help. If you are broke, do both inner work and outer work.

- Spiritual bypassing is using positive language to avoid real feelings, real conversations, or real problems. "Good vibes only" is how people suppress grief, avoid hard conversations, and ignore warning signs. The practice includes real work with what is here, including the heavy things.

## A weekly practice

- **Morning:** five minutes of appreciation, five minutes of scripting, one intention for the day.
- **Midday:** pause, check state. What am I vibrating? Shift one step up the scale.
- **Evening:** notice one thing that arrived — a synchronicity, an opening, a person who said the right thing. Write it down.
- **Weekly:** re-read the scripting. Adjust the clarity. Take the inspired action that keeps appearing.

Keep this light. Over-efforting at manifestation is itself a form of resistance.
