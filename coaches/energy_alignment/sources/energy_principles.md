# Energy Alignment — Principles & Practice

## What "energy" means here

Energy in this work is a practical term for the felt, moving, quality-ful dimension of your present state. Your physical sensation, emotional tone, breath, attention, and subtle sense of presence — taken as one flowing thing.

You can hold this as a metaphysical reality (life force, chi, prana, subtle energy) or as the integrated felt experience of a living nervous system. Either way, the practices work because they change what the body and nervous system are actually doing.

## The body is the instrument

You cannot shift your energy while staying in your head. Thinking about your energy does not change your energy. Attention to sensation does. The first and last move in any energy practice is returning to the body.

- Where am I? (literally — what is my physical location)
- What is touching me? (the chair, the floor, the air, clothing)
- What is the temperature? The quality of light?
- What is my breath doing, right now, before I change it?

Two or three minutes of this resets the nervous system. Every practice below builds on this foundation.

## Grounding

Grounding is the practice of dropping your attention and your sense of self into the body and into the earth. It is the antidote to scattered, buzzy, dissociated, anxious, or overwhelmed states.

Simple grounding:
- Feet flat on the floor. Feel the floor pressing up into your soles.
- Exhale longer than you inhale, three times.
- Imagine roots growing down from your feet into the earth, through soil and stone, all the way to the core.
- Let whatever you are carrying that is not yours drain down those roots.
- Draw up from the earth whatever you need — steadiness, warmth, patience.

Five minutes of this before an important conversation changes the conversation.

## Clearing

Clearing is the practice of releasing what is stuck, absorbed, or heavy — emotion that did not move through, weight picked up from environments or people, old patterns ready to go.

Simple clearing:
- **Breath clearing:** long slow exhales, imagining you are releasing something specific (the meeting, the conversation, the day). Make the exhale audible if it helps.
- **Water clearing:** a shower with intention. As water moves over you, imagine it carrying away what you are ready to release. Simple. Old. Effective.
- **Movement clearing:** shake the body for 2–3 minutes. Arms, hands, legs, whole body. This is a nervous system reset practised across traditions.
- **Writing to clear:** write what you're holding until the page is full, then close the book. You do not need to reread.

## Centring

Centring is the practice of returning from the scattered edges of experience to your core. When attention is pulled outward to other people's moods, to your phone, to the task list, centring brings it home.

Simple centring:
- Hand on the heart, hand on the belly.
- Three slow breaths. Feel the heart under one hand. Feel the belly under the other.
- Sense the vertical line between them — the axis of the body.
- Let attention come to rest along that axis.

Thirty seconds, repeatable throughout the day. Useful before any meaningful transition.

## Raising

Raising is for low, heavy, depleted states — when you are not overwhelmed or scattered but flat, dull, or tired.

Simple raising:
- Stand up. Physiology matters.
- Breath in through the nose slightly faster than usual. Full belly, full chest, slight pause. Exhale easily.
- Move: walking, stretching, a song played loudly that you loved as a teenager.
- Sun if you can get it. Cold water on the face if you can't.
- Something warm: tea, a wool blanket, a hot shower.

This isn't forced positivity. It is giving the body the inputs it needs to come back online.

## The chakras as a working map

The seven main chakras are a map used across yogic and tantric traditions. Take them as pointers to regions of human experience that can be worked with directly:

1. **Root (base of spine) — safety, belonging, survival, home.**
   When off: anxiety, feeling unrooted, financial stress, restlessness.
   Practice: grounding, contact with earth, routine, weight on the body, feeling your feet.

2. **Sacral (low belly) — pleasure, creativity, flow, sensuality.**
   When off: flatness, creative block, shutdown sexuality, rigidity.
   Practice: movement that swings and flows (dance, hips), water, creative play, sensual pleasure (food, touch, beauty).

3. **Solar plexus (upper belly) — will, power, agency, action.**
   When off: collapse, people-pleasing, loss of clarity about what you want, avoidance.
   Practice: fire — warmth, sunlight, strong exhalation; saying what you mean; small decisive action.

4. **Heart (chest) — love, connection, compassion, grief.**
   When off: armoured, cut off, resentful, unable to receive.
   Practice: hand on heart, long slow breath into the chest, contact with someone you love, letting grief be grief.

5. **Throat (neck) — expression, truth, voice.**
   When off: unspoken things, held tension in jaw and neck, chronic throat-clearing, swallowed anger.
   Practice: singing, humming, speaking aloud what is true, writing what cannot be said, giving voice.

6. **Third eye (forehead) — insight, vision, seeing clearly.**
   When off: confusion, overthinking, disconnect between what you know and what you do.
   Practice: rest, darkness, contemplation, attention to intuition, quiet before decisions.

7. **Crown (top of head) — meaning, connection to something larger.**
   When off: nihilism, going through the motions, spiritual thirst unacknowledged.
   Practice: awe (sky, music, art), contemplation, ritual, time in nature.

You don't need to believe in chakras as entities to use this map. It organises human experience into workable regions. When something feels off, it often points clearly to one of these areas.

## Your energy vs other people's

Much of what people call being drained, triggered, or depressed is energy they have absorbed from environments, conversations, or people. Not all of your state is yours.

After difficult people or noisy environments, a deliberate clearing practice matters. Otherwise the weight sits.

- After a hard conversation: walk, wash hands, shake the body.
- After a draining day: shower with intention, change clothes.
- Before sleep: release the day in some form.

This is not about rejecting others. It is about not carrying other people's state as your own.

## Boundaries and containment

Some people have a naturally open field. They feel everything. This is a gift and a liability.

Containment practices help:
- Imagine a membrane around your field — not a wall, a permeable but clear boundary. What is yours stays in. What is not yours stays out.
- Before entering difficult spaces (hospital, argument, crowded event), consciously set the boundary.
- After leaving, re-check it.

Boundaries are not about fear. They are about knowing where you end and where others begin.

## Flow vs stagnation

Energy wants to move. When it is flowing, you feel alive, responsive, clear. When it is stagnant, you feel heavy, dull, stuck. The cures for stagnation are almost always some form of movement — physical, emotional, relational, creative.

If you are stuck, ask: what hasn't moved here? What is being held? What wants to come out? The answer is rarely subtle.

## Honest caveats

- Energy work is not medical treatment. If something is physically wrong, see a doctor. The body knows — listen to it when it asks for real help.
- Energy work is not psychotherapy. For trauma, chronic depression, persistent anxiety, see a therapist. These tools can be supportive alongside real treatment; they are not a replacement.
- Do not diagnose others from their aura, energy, or chakras. You do not know what is going on in another person's body and spirit from a distance.
- Beware of spiritual framing that keeps you stuck. "It's my Saturn return" is sometimes useful, but sometimes it is a way to avoid doing the practical thing that would actually help.

## A daily practice

- **Morning:** three minutes of grounding before you pick up the phone.
- **Midday:** thirty seconds of centring at a doorway.
- **Evening:** a clearing practice — shower, walk, writing, or breath — before sleep.

Ten minutes total. Done most days, it is more than enough.
