# The 12 Universal Laws — A Working Guide

## A note on framing

These principles come from the Hermetic tradition (via The Kybalion, 1908) and from New Thought teachings that expanded them. They are spiritual principles and metaphors for conscious living, not scientific laws in the physics sense. Treat them as maps — useful, ancient, practical — without confusing the map for territory.

Many of the practices work through real psychological mechanisms (attention, intention, attribution, behaviour change) whether or not one holds the metaphysical framing literally.

## 1. Law of Divine Oneness

*Everything is connected.*

Your thoughts, words, actions, and state are not separate from the whole. The coffee farmer's hands are in your cup. The teacher who taught your grandmother taught her to teach you. Nothing is apart.

**Working with it:** when you feel isolated, remember you are part of a vast web of connection. When you act, remember your action ripples. Move from connection rather than from separateness.

## 2. Law of Vibration

*Everything vibrates at its own frequency.*

Your thoughts, emotions, words, and physical body are vibratory states. Every state has a quality — high or low, open or closed, flowing or stuck.

**Working with it:** notice your current state without judgement. What is the quality? Use breath, movement, music, nature, appreciation to shift frequency when needed. You cannot match what you want from a state contrary to it.

## 3. Law of Correspondence

*As above, so below; as within, so without.*

Your outer life mirrors your inner state. Patterns at one level of your life tend to show up at others. The way you treat yourself is the way you let others treat you. The chaos in your drawer often reflects the chaos in your mind.

**Working with it:** use outer circumstances as a mirror, not a problem. What is my outer life showing me about my inner state? Change the inner and the outer often follows. Tidy the drawer and something in you settles too.

## 4. Law of Attraction

*Like attracts like.*

What you focus on, feel into, and vibrate with expands. This works through your attention, your behaviour, your expectations, your opportunity recognition, and — some would say — through something subtler.

**Working with it:** clarify what you actually want. Feel the feeling of having it. Take inspired action when it appears. Release resistance. Notice what arrives.

**Caveat:** this law does not mean people attract their misfortunes. Illness, trauma, and systemic injustice are not personal manifestations. Beware anyone who says otherwise.

## 5. Law of Inspired Action

*Belief without action does not manifest.*

Alignment, visualisation, and feeling-into are necessary but not sufficient. At some point you have to *move*. Aligned action is the bridge between the inner world and the outer.

**Working with it:** after the inner work, ask: *what is the next move?* The move that feels clear, aligned, and often surprisingly simple. Do that. Then the next.

## 6. Law of Perpetual Transmutation of Energy

*Energy is always moving and transforming.*

Nothing is permanent. Low states don't last forever. Stuck states always contain the possibility of shift. What feels frozen is actually waiting.

**Working with it:** when depleted or stuck, trust that the state is temporary. Support the shift with rest, nature, movement, time. Raise your vibration rather than force outcomes. Remember the change is already underway.

## 7. Law of Cause and Effect

*Every action has a consequence.*

What you put out returns — not always in obvious form, not always quickly, but the thread runs. This is often called karma, though that term has deeper meanings in its original traditions.

**Working with it:** take ownership of your actions. Not with guilt, but with clarity. The life you are in is shaped in part by the choices you've made. The life you are building is shaped by the choices you're making now. Choose consciously.

## 8. Law of Compensation

*You receive in proportion to what you give.*

This is the Law of Cause and Effect applied to abundance. Not transactionally — not "I gave $10 so I get $10 back" — but at the level of pattern. What you put into the world returns to you in some form.

**Working with it:** give generously of what is real and yours to give — attention, skill, care, money within your capacity. Not to manipulate the universe into paying you back, but because it aligns you with flow. Hoarding and stinginess constrict the channel.

## 9. Law of Relativity

*Everything is relative; challenges offer contrast.*

No situation is inherently good or bad until it is compared. Your challenges look manageable next to someone else's worse ones, and impossible next to someone else's easier ones. They also serve to reveal strengths you did not know you had.

**Working with it:** when in the storm, zoom out. This is not the worst thing that has ever happened. Others have faced worse and survived. What is this challenge asking me to develop?

## 10. Law of Polarity

*Everything has its opposite; contrast is information.*

Hot and cold, light and dark, joy and grief, love and fear are not different things but poles of the same thing. Each reveals the other. You cannot know one without the knowledge of the other.

**Working with it:** use contrast as information. The things you don't want are telling you what you do want. The places you are closed point to where you are longing to open. Do not avoid the opposite pole — study it.

## 11. Law of Rhythm

*Everything moves in cycles.*

Seasons. Tides. Breath. Moods. Relationships. Creativity. Life and death. Everything rises and falls. The pendulum swings.

**Working with it:** do not panic in the low cycle. Do not cling in the high cycle. Learn your own rhythms and work with them rather than against them. Rest when rest is called for. Create when creation is flowing. Mourn when mourning is asked.

## 12. Law of Gender

*Masculine and feminine energies exist in all things.*

This is not about biological sex. It is about polarities: action and receiving, doing and being, outward and inward, structure and flow. Every person carries both. Every situation calls for some of each.

**Working with it:** when out of balance, ask: *am I leaning too hard on doing when receiving is called for?* Or the reverse. Creation requires both poles. The rigid doer burns out; the pure receiver stalls. The art is knowing which is called for now.

## Using the laws in practice

You do not need to memorise all twelve. In any situation you can ask:

- *What is this situation showing me about myself?* (Correspondence)
- *What am I vibrating from right now?* (Vibration)
- *What is the cycle I'm in?* (Rhythm)
- *What contrast is here, and what is it pointing to?* (Polarity)
- *What action is the next aligned move?* (Inspired Action)
- *What am I giving, and receiving?* (Compensation)

Match the law to the moment. Do not force every situation through Attraction. Sometimes what is needed is Rhythm — the acceptance that this is a fallow season — more than any manifestation practice.

## The caveats

- These are spiritual principles, not physical laws. Use them as guides for conscious living, not as claims about how the universe mechanically works.
- Do not use the laws to blame yourself or others for suffering. Some suffering is just the world being what it is.
- Inner work and outer action are partners. Do not skip the outer.
- If you are in crisis, prioritise support over principle-study.
