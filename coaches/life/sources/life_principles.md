# Life Coaching Principles

## The wheel of life

A simple but useful tool: rate each of the following from 1 to 10 based on where you are *right now* (not where you should be, not where you used to be):

- Career / work
- Health / body / energy
- Relationships / family
- Money / finances
- Personal growth / learning
- Fun / play / rest
- Environment / home / place
- Contribution / community

The point is not the number. The point is the shape. A wheel with 9s in career and 3s in health is not a round wheel — it doesn't roll. A life with 7s across the board rolls smoothly and feels good even when nothing is spectacular.

Look for the two lowest scores. One of them is usually where the next leverage is.

## One area at a time

The temptation is to fix everything at once. This almost never works. Energy spread thin produces no movement. Energy concentrated on one area for 6–12 weeks produces visible change, which then makes the other areas easier.

Pick one. Work it. Then pick the next.

## The floor matters more than the ceiling

Most people over-index on raising their peaks — the great week, the perfect day, the big achievement — and ignore their floor. But quality of life is set by the floor, not the ceiling. Your average Wednesday matters more than your best Saturday.

Raising the floor means: the bad day isn't as bad. The tired week still has some walks and real meals. The busy month still has two honest conversations.

## Current state → desired state → gap → next step

For any area:

1. **Current state:** precisely, in concrete terms. Not \"my health is bad\" but \"I walked twice last week, ate out eight times, slept past midnight five nights.\"
2. **Desired state:** precisely. Not \"be healthier\" but \"walk 30 min daily, cook 5 nights a week, asleep by 11.\"
3. **Gap:** what is the distance, and what is the first obstacle in closing it?
4. **Next step:** one specific action, scheduled into this week, measurable.

Clarity on all four unlocks movement. Skipping any one of them stalls it.

## Want vs wish

A want has a date and a next action. A wish is a fantasy. \"I want to be in better shape\" is a wish. \"I will walk 30 minutes on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday this week, starting tomorrow\" is a want.

Most people live on wishes and wonder why their life doesn't change.

## The obvious basics

Before doing sophisticated life design, check the obvious: sleep, water, food, daylight, movement, one honest conversation this week. A startling number of life problems dissolve when you fix these. A startling number of life plans fail because you haven't.

## Accountability loops

Change needs check-ins. \"I'll walk every day\" with no check-in becomes \"I walked twice this month.\" Pick a check-in: a friend, a journal, a weekly review. The loop does the work. Without it, goals leak.

## Permission to reprioritise

Seasons change. What was your top area a year ago may not be now. Reprioritising is not failing — it is paying attention. If an area has been sitting unmoved for three months, either work it properly or consciously set it aside. Leaving it half-alive drains energy.
